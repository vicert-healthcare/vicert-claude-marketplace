Nas par je pricalo o tome kako proceniti gde i kada se koristi AI first solutions u health tech-u, i da li postoji framework koji nam moze pomoci da ocenimo odredjeni use case, evo sta smo dobili:


🧠 **Question:**
&gt; **Is there a framework or tool that can assess the potential for applying artificial intelligence (AI - first ) to solve a specific problem in healthcare or health-tech?**
💬 **Answer:**
Yes — there are several frameworks that help you evaluate whether AI is an appropriate and valuable solution for a specific healthcare or health-tech problem. While there isn’t yet a fully automated public tool where you can just “type in your problem” and get a yes/no answer, there are structured **assessment frameworks** that can guide you through the decision process.

:white*check*mark: **Existing Frameworks and References**
• **American College of Cardiology – AI in Health Care: Key Assessment Criteria**
 A practical checklist for defining goals, assessing data quality, regulatory implications, and clinical value before developing an AI solution.
 :point*right: https://www.acc.org/-/media/Non-Clinical/Files-PDFs-Excel-MS-Word-etc/About-ACC/Innovation/AI/AI-in-Health-Care-Tool*Key-Assessment-Criteria.pdf

• **Michigan Health &amp; Hospital Association – AI Framework for Healthcare**
Provides strategic and ethical guidance for integrating AI into healthcare workflows responsibly.
 :point*right: https://www.mha.org/wp-content/uploads/2024/12/AI-Framework-for-Healthcare*MHA-AI-Taskforce.pdf

• **Stanford Health Care – FURM (Fair, Useful, Reliable Model) Framework**
 Used internally at Stanford to evaluate whether an AI use case is meaningful, ethical, and sustainable before deployment.
 :point*right: https://shahlab.stanford.edu/lib/exe/fetch.php?media=furm*paper*-*main*appendix*submitted*to*arxiv.pdf

• **FUTURE-AI Consortium – International Guidelines**
A cross-institutional initiative defining trustworthy AI principles for medical applications (fairness, usability, reproducibility, robustness).
👉 https://www.bmj.com/content/388/bmj-2024-081554

⚙️ **How Such a System Could Work**
If you were to build or adapt a tool for this, it could function like an **interactive assessment engine**. The system would ask questions and then generate a recommendation such as:

• :white*check*mark: *“AI is a strong candidate for this problem.”*
• ⚠️ *“AI could help, but further evaluation is needed.”*
• ❌ *“AI is not recommended for this use case right now.”*
The key assessment areas usually include:
1. **Problem Definition** – What exactly are you trying to improve (clinical outcome, workflow, cost, patient experience)?
2. **Data and Infrastructure** – Are relevant, high-quality datasets available? Are they standardized and accessible?
3. **Clinical and Operational Value** – Does AI add unique value over existing methods?
4. **Regulatory and Ethical Considerations** – Are there privacy, bias, or safety risks?
5. **Resources and Sustainability** – Do you have the team, budget, and infrastructure to maintain the system?
6. **Integration and Adoption** – Will the AI tool fit into existing clinical workflows and be accepted by users?
💡 **Summary**
While no plug-and-play AI feasibility checker for healthcare exists yet, these frameworks give you a **clear roadmap** to evaluate whether your idea has strong AI potential. They can easily be turned into a scoring or decision tool inside a web app, chatbot, or Slack workflow.
