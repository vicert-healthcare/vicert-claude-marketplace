**Reply from Bojan Todorovic:**
Of course, here it is:

**TLDR:**
Cursor 2.0 merges the speed of Cursor 1.0 with the parallel workflows of Claude Code—and it's rewriting how AI builders work. The new Composer model is 4x faster, parallel agents let you compare multiple AI solutions simultaneously, and multi-session agents finally let you run different tasks at once without context switching. The built-in browser integration and ChatGPT-like Agent View make it accessible to non-coders for the first time.

**The catch?** It breaks things faster when you're wrong, context persistence took a hit, and merge conflicts from parallel agents require manual cleanup. After a week of stress-testing it on production systems, productivity stayed flat—but the workflow feels fundamentally different. Work that took months compressed into weeks. The tool isn't perfect, but it's the future: you're not iterating with AI anymore, you're curating from it.

**Bottom line:** Despite the friction, there's no going back. Cursor 2.0 isn't competing with IDEs—it's competing with your entire AI toolchain.
