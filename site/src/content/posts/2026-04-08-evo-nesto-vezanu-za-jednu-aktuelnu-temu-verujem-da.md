Evo nesto vezanu za jednu aktuelnu temu, verujem da ste svi vec culi za Artemis II :slightly*smiling*face:

🚀 ***Artemis II is heading back from the Moon and AI was along for the ride***

In case you missed it, NASA launched 4 astronauts around the Moon on April 1st (yes, really, not an April Fools joke). They completed the lunar flyby on April 6th and are now on their way home, splashing down on April 10th.
What's cool for us in this channel: AI quietly played a bigger role in this mission than most people realize:

:robot*face: *AI as the "5th crew member"_ - Unlike Apollo-era software, Artemis II uses AI dynamically to monitor life support and trajectory, not just execute fixed commands.

:satellite*antenna: *On-board autonomy_ - During the lunar flyby, the crew lost contact with Earth for up to 50 minutes. AI systems on Orion were handling anomaly detection and responding in real-time, no Houston needed.

:car: *Bonus: AI already drives on Mars* - Just months ago in December 2025, NASA's Perseverance rover completed the first drives on another planet planned *entirely* by generative AI. No human route planners involved. 28 years of manual driving, gone.

:crystal*ball: *What's next_ - NASA plans autonomous AI-powered construction robots on the Moon's surface by 2028.

**And the best part?** 😂😂😂
💻 *240,000 miles from Earth... and Microsoft Outlook still wouldn't open.* Commander Wiseman had to call Mission Control to remote into his laptop and fix it. Mission Control confirmed: "We were able to get it open. It will show offline, which is expected."
Some things are truly universal. :saluting_face:
