Just wanted to drop two AI security articles in case you haven't already stumbled upon them.
• [The first one](https://blog.trailofbits.com/2025/08/21/weaponizing-image-scaling-against-production-ai-systems/) (published seven months ago) covers a technique where attackers craft an image that looks completely normal to the human eye, but when an AI system scales it down, a hidden prompt is revealed that the model can execute as an instruction. 
• [The second one](https://www.microsoft.com/en-us/security/blog/2026/02/10/ai-recommendation-poisoning/) is from the Microsoft Security team (published last month) and covers what they call "AI Recommendation Poisoning", where websites embed hidden instructions in "Summarize with AI" buttons that, when clicked, manipulate your AI assistant's memory to favor their products or services in future responses.
