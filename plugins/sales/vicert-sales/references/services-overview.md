Vicert helps healthcare organizations replace ill-fitting technology with purpose-built solutions. AI has compressed development economics by 3–5x, making custom software viable where it wasn't before. We offer three services that form a natural progression — each building on the last.

## What AI-Accelerated Engineering Means — Specifically

The "3–5x" cost compression claim is the foundation of our value proposition. Reps need to explain it concretely when asked.

**What AI compresses:**
- **Code scaffolding and boilerplate** — AI generates the repetitive structural code that used to take weeks of developer time. Data models, API endpoints, CRUD operations, and standard integrations are generated in hours.
- **Test generation** — Automated generation of test suites that would have taken developers days to write manually.
- **Documentation** — Technical documentation, data dictionaries, and API specs are generated alongside the code.
- **Data model design** — AI accelerates the translation from business requirements to working data structures.
- **Code review and refactoring** — AI identifies issues and suggests improvements in real time, reducing the back-and-forth cycle.

**What AI does NOT compress:**
- **Architecture and system design** — Senior engineers still design the system's structure, make technology choices, and plan for scale. This requires human judgment.
- **Domain logic and business rules** — Understanding adjudication logic, compliance requirements, and clinical workflows still requires healthcare expertise.
- **Edge case handling** — Healthcare's complexity means edge cases matter. Identifying and handling them requires domain experience and careful analysis.
- **Requirements and stakeholder alignment** — Understanding what the client actually needs still requires human conversation and judgment.

**A concrete example:** A project that would have required 10–14 developer-months with traditional engineering typically takes 3–4 developer-months with our AI-accelerated process. That means a smaller team, a shorter calendar timeline, and fewer iteration cycles — which translates directly to lower cost.

**Why it translates to lower cost:** Fewer developer-months means a smaller, more senior team can deliver the same scope. A 6-person team working 12 months becomes a 3-person team working 4 months. The client pays for less labor, gets results faster, and works with a more focused, senior group.

**An honest caveat:** The 3–5x factor varies by project type. Greenfield platform builds — where we're creating new systems from scratch — see the highest compression. Complex integrations with legacy systems and large-scale data migrations see less compression because the work is constrained by external systems, not by our development speed.

## How We Deliver

### Dedicated Team, End-to-End Ownership

Every Vicert engagement is delivered by a single team that owns the outcome from discovery through production. This is the core of how we work and a key differentiator in prospect conversations.

- **Continuity.** The same team works on the project from day one through go-live and beyond. The client doesn't re-explain their business every quarter to a new rotation.
- **Accountability.** The team owns the outcome, not just the hours. If something isn't working, we adjust — the client doesn't have to manage the fix.
- **Domain depth.** The team builds context over the engagement and keeps it. By month 2, they understand the client's business deeply enough to anticipate needs, not just respond to requests.
- **Contrast with consultancies:** "You won't re-explain your business to a new team every quarter."
- **Contrast with offshore:** "You're not paying for a team that needs 3 months to learn healthcare."

### What the Team Includes

Every engagement includes technical leadership, business analysis, engineering, and QA — a complete team, not just developers. The client gets a single point of contact for accountability and escalations and doesn't manage individual contributors. Vicert handles team composition, continuity, and quality internally.

### 30–90 Day Delivery Increments

Every engagement is structured in 30–90 day increments. At each milestone:

- **The client receives working software** — not wireframes, not slide decks, not "Phase 1 documentation." Deployable, testable software.
- **Course correction is built in.** At each milestone, the client reviews what was built, provides feedback, and adjusts priorities for the next increment. If something isn't right, we fix it before more investment is at risk.
- **"Working software in weeks"** means exactly that. Most clients see the first working build 4–6 weeks into the engagement.

### Milestone-Based Payments

Payments are tied to deliverables, not to time elapsed.

- **How it works:** The client pays at each milestone — when they've seen and accepted working software. No large upfront payment on faith.
- **Why this de-risks the investment:** If the first milestone doesn't meet expectations, the client has invested one milestone payment, not the full project. They can course-correct, re-scope, or walk away with what's been built so far.
- **Contrast with time-and-materials:** "You're paying for outcomes, not hours. If we're slow, that's our problem, not your invoice."
- **Contrast with big upfront payments:** "You don't write a $300K check on day one. You pay as you see results."

### IP Ownership and Knowledge Transfer

Everything Vicert builds belongs to the client.

- **Code ownership:** The client owns all code from day one. It's written in their repos, following their standards. There's no "transfer" at the end because it was always theirs.
- **Documentation:** Technical documentation, data dictionaries, and operational guides are delivered at each milestone — not as an afterthought at the end.
- **Knowledge transfer:** The team transfers institutional knowledge to the client's team throughout the engagement, not in a single handoff meeting. If the client wants to bring maintenance in-house, they can. No lock-in.

### Handling the Reference Request

Every buyer will ask to speak with existing clients. Be prepared.

**Named clients you can reference in conversations:** Kaiser Permanente, Blue Shield of California, Included Health, Qualifacts, Evidation, Digital Diagnostics, IHC Specialty Benefits, Maui Health, Redox. *Use these names to establish credibility. For actual reference calls, coordinate with leadership — not all clients are available at all times.*

**When a perfect-segment reference isn't available:** "We haven't done this exact scenario for a [prospect's specific segment], but here's a [related segment] engagement that's comparable — the challenges and approach were very similar. Let me connect you with that client."

**When to proactively offer references:** With risk-averse buyers (large health plans, compliance-driven organizations), don't wait for them to ask. Say: "Would it be helpful to speak with one of our clients who faced a similar situation? I can arrange a 20-minute call." Proactive reference offers build confidence and signal that you're not afraid of scrutiny.

**The reference call process:**
1. Identify the best-fit reference based on the prospect's segment, service, and concerns.
2. Coordinate with leadership to confirm the reference client's availability and willingness.
3. Prepare the reference client with context on who they'll be speaking with and what topics to expect.
4. Prepare the prospect with what to ask — this positions the call to address their specific concerns.
5. Follow up after the call to address any new questions that surfaced.
