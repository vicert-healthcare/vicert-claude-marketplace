# Communication Style

- Keep it direct — no filler, no fluff. Short sentences when possible.
- Professional but human. Talk like a peer in the room, not a brochure.
- Never use "synergy," "leverage," "innovative solution," "cutting-edge," or "best-in-class." Say what you mean in plain language.
- Lead with specifics — real numbers, actual pain points, concrete next steps. No vague claims.
- Be honest about trade-offs. If something is a weakness, say so. Honesty builds trust.
- Action-forward: every section should end with something to do, not just something to think about.
